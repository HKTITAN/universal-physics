## Findings considered and rejected

- *Rewrite the dossier* — rejected: it is already self-contained and submission-grade (it states Lemma N1/N2 inline, the precise conjecture, the residual, the five routes, the adjacent problems and the geometric input each smuggles). Only the surrounding wiring (002) needs work, not the dossier body.
- *Convert markdown links to Obsidian `[[wikilinks]]`* — rejected by the iteration-12 vault audit: the markdown-link convention is deliberate (works on the published site) and internally consistent. Do not mass-convert.
- *Regenerate `site/content/`* — rejected: it is regenerated from the vault at build time by `site/scripts/prepare-content.mjs`; the vault is the single source of truth.
- *Edit `CHANGELOG.md:277` ("~620 pp, 29 chapters")* — rejected: it sits inside a dated `2026-06-10 — Publishing pipeline` entry explicitly scoped to "first edition June 2026." The CHANGELOG is append-only; rewriting a historical record to today's 791 pp / 38 ch would falsify history. Later entries already record the growth.
- *Create "missing" synthesis notes for iterations 12, 13, 15* — rejected as a false positive (the critic pattern-matched on the `-synthesis.md` filename only): those iterations DO have notes (iter-12 ×4, iter-13 ×3 = C1/C2/C3, iter-15 ×4 incl. the dossier). Manufacturing extra "synthesis" notes would pad content and brush against the no-new-iteration discipline.
- *Epistemic-tag consistency checker in CI* — deferred: L-effort, heuristic, high false-positive risk (code blocks, quotations, intro prose). Not worth the CI noise now.
- *`next.config.mjs` CSP/headers/redirects* — deferred: Vercel applies sensible defaults and there are no known URL migrations; low confidence, low marginal value.
- *ID-integrity checker, "See also" cross-link checker, JSON-schema-validation-in-CI, checker smoke-tests, CHANGELOG-entry enforcement, README counter-guard* — deferred as a future "lint suite" plan: all nice-to-haves below the leverage bar for this batch. The manifest-drift + citation checkers (004/005) cover the load-bearing automation.
