## 4. The single calibrated paragraph (the deliverable)

> **How close are we to a universal theory of physics?** It depends entirely on which question you mean. On the most modest reading — *could a single consistent framework joining general relativity and quantum field theory exist?* — we are **close, and the news is good**: none of the seventeen catalogued GR–QFT clashes is a proven logical inconsistency (they are domain-mismatches and unsolved-but-consistent problems living where both theories are extrapolated past their tested regimes), and AdS/CFT furnishes a worked, internally-consistent example that *some* consistent quantum gravity exists — though, to be exact, AdS/CFT is a strongly-evidenced *conjecture*, not a proven theorem, so this is a demonstration-by-example, not a closed mathematical case. On the more demanding reading — *do we have a specific theory, derived rather than merely encoded, and is it the best-developed candidate?* — we are **not close, but the gap is unusually well-localized**: the leading direction (causal/algebraic/entanglement structure preceding metric geometry) *encodes* geometry without *generating* it, and the entire distance between "encodes" and "generates" sits at one precisely-stated bottleneck — the carrier problem (equivalently a reverse-Weinberg–Witten theorem, equivalently a net-naturality theorem), the single step at which all five known constructions close, and a problem now gated on new operator-algebra mathematics whose nearest named neighbour, Connes' bicentralizer problem, is itself open as of 2026. On the decisive reading — *is there an experiment that bears on this central wager?* — the answer is **none, even in principle**: by the encoding screen an encode-only program predicts exactly what the effective field theory it encodes predicts, so the only discriminating test would be a "reverse Weinberg–Witten" observable that no one has constructed, and which is in fact the carrier problem wearing experimental clothes; every funded experiment that exists — gravity-mediated entanglement (gate), objective-collapse bounds now tightened by XENONnT (core), and DESI's evolving-dark-energy hint (target) — probes the program's prerequisites, foundations, or destination, but never the wager itself, and the nearest dated decision point, DESI's five-year equation-of-state verdict (now realistically 2027–2028, the survey having been extended to late 2028), would adjudicate which de Sitter–like cosmology we inhabit, not whether geometry is emergent. The honest meta-conclusion — and the genuinely load-bearing one — is that on the two axes that matter, progress no longer comes from reasoning: the analytical program is at terminal saturation, every verdict-bearing residual is decidable only by external input, and the calibrated stance is therefore neither the false optimism of an imminent "theory of everything" nor the false despair of a closed door, but a sharp, conservative *not-yet*: a consistent framework is plausibly possible, a derived theory is one located theorem away, that theorem is currently unreachable by argument alone, and no experiment yet conceived can referee the wager that the whole programme rests on. **[INFERENCE, high; the (b)/(c) horizon estimates SPECULATIVE; the AdS/CFT and DESI calibration caveats web-verified.]**
